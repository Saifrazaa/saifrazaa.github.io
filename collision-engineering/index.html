BEGIN A CAREER IN COLLISION ENGINEERING

Imagine working in an industry where your passion for anything on four wheels pays off. Where you can earn while you learn from industry experts. And where you can set yourself up for a successful and lucrative career. Imagine using cutting-edge computer technology to repair vintage vehicles and hybrid models. Now imagine doing all that in a clean, safe space surrounded by a team that shares your enthusiasm, helps you find answers to complex challenges, and supports you as you grow and develop.

This is what a career in automotive collision engineering can offer. That, and so much more

Course Structure

Typically, you'll spend your first eight weeks in the classroom at your chosen school, learning through lecturos and hands on skills training. Then you'11 begin a work rotation at a participating collision repair center for another eight weeks, where you'll put everything you learned in the classroom into practice in an exciting, real work environment.

What is a collision engineering

With vehicles constantly advancing, the modern technician must possess the ingenuity to understand and restore these highly sophisticated vehicles and their technologies. A Collision Engineer is someone with passion, a strong work ethic, and a willingness to learn. To be a Collision Engineer is a goal that all technicians must strive for. A lifelong learner who advances alongside progressing technologies and meets the ever- changing needs of the industry.
